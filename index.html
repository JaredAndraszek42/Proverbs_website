Welcome to Jared's notes on Proverbs

Proverbs is the 20th book of the Bible. It focuses on the wisdom bestowed to King Solomon (the greatest king of Israel) by God. This website takes the highlights of each chapter (31) and explains how I interpret them. Every day, there will be a new chapter added until 3/31. I also want it to be known that I use the NIV (New International Version) translation, so all quotes use that version.

Chapter 1

Chapter 1 starts with why wisdom is important and who this wisdom is from. It is stated that Solomon, the son of David, King of Israel recieved wisdom from the Lord in 1 Kings 3:12. These proverbs are the wisdom that Solomon recieved and is sharing to the world.

For it is written:
"1The proverbs of Solomon son of David, king of Israel: 2for attaining wisdom and discipline; for understanding words of insight; 3for acquiring a disciplined and prudent life, doing what is right and just and fair; " (Proverbs 1:1-3)

We are then told that noone young or old should stop looking to wisdom and to continue our learning. As the young learn to grow and the old learn to guide.

For it is written:
"4for giving prudence to the simple, knowledge and discretion to the young- 5let the wise listen and add to their learning, and let the discerning get guidance- 6for understanding proverbs and parables, the sayings and riddles of the wise" (Proverbs 1:4-6)

We then are told our first piece of wisdom and that is "7The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge, but fools despise wisdom and discipline." So we must start with fearing the lord and the judgement we face for our sins before we can truely start learning and becoming wise in the eyes of God.

The next section of Chapter 1 speaks about embracing wisdom and a warning against enticement.
We start with a call for us to listen to what is stated in verses 8 and 9.

For it is written:
"8Listen, my son, to your father's instruction and do not forsake your mother's teaching. 9They will be a garland to grace your head and a chain to adorn your neck." (Proverbs 1:8-9)

We then transition to not giving into the sinners of the world with their murder, theft, and ignorance.

For it is written:
"10My son, if sinners entice you, do not give in to them. 11If they say, 'Come along with us: let's lie in wait for someone's blood, let's waylay some harmless soul; 12let's swallow them alive like the grave, and whole, like those who go down to the pit; 13we will get all sorts of valuable things and fill our houses with plunder; 14throw in your lot with us, and we will share a common purse'-" (Proverbs 1:10-14)

We then are told not to go with them, as they rush to sin and shed blood to where they end up shedding themselves as they are then the targets of sinners. So do not take the ill-gotten gains, as anyone who does will die. We are also told in verse 17 that even the animals are smart enough to not fall for a trap when the hunter is setting it up.

For it is written:
"15my son, do not go along with them, do not set foot on their paths; 16for their feet rush into sin, they are swift to shed blood. 17How useless to spread a net in full view of all the birds 18These men lie in wait for their own blood; they waylay only themselves! 19Such is the end of all who go after ill-gotten gain; it takes away the lives of those who get it." (Proverbs 1:15-19)

However, if we fail to embrace wisdom, we are given a warning against rejecting Wisdom. We start with Wisdom, personified as a woman/mother figure calling out to everyone in the streets, public squares, and other public areas offering knowledge to those who want it.

For it is written:
"20Wisdom calls aloud in the street, she raises her voice in the public squares; 21at the head of the noisy streets she cries out, in the gateways of the city she makes her speech: 22'How long will you simple ones love your simple ways? How long will mockers delight in mockery and fools hate knowledge?" (Proverbs 1:20-22)

However, if we refuse Wisdom's knowledge, Wisdom will turn her back on those that refused it when difficult times come.

For it is written:
"23If you had responded to my rebuke, I would have poured out my heart to you and made my thoughts known to you. 24But since you rejected me when I called and no one gave heet when I stretched out my hand, 25since you ignored all my advice and would not accept my rebuke, 26I in turn will laugh at your disaster; I will mock when calamity overtakes you- 27when calamity overtakes you like a storm, when disaster sweeps over you like a whirlwind, when distress and trouble overwhelm you." (Proverbs 1:23-27)

At this point those that refused Wisdom will realize the mistake they made and will call out for help from Wisdom, but she will not be there. Because they chose not to fear the Lord and chose not to have knowledge, they chose not to have the safety and ease Wisdom offered.

For it is written:
"28Then they will call to me but I will not answer; they will look for me but will not find me. 29Since they hated knowledge and did not choose to fear the LORD, 30since they would not accept my advice and spurned my rebuke, 31they will eat the fruit of their ways and be filled with the fruit of their schemes. 32For the waywardmess of the simple will kill them, and the complacency of fools will destroy them; 33but whoever listens to me will live in safety and be at ease, without fear of harm."
